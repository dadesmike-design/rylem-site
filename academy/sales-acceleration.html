🤝 Running Discovery Meetings
💬 The Discovery Meeting Framework
💡
80/20 Rule

The more THEY talk, the better the meeting went. Your job is to ask questions and listen. Always show up early. Always ask for a job order.

🎯Meeting Opening Script▼
"Thank you for taking the time. If you ever need to hire a contractor, Rylem would love to help. That said, I'm not here to sell — I just want to learn about you and your group."

Set the tone: you're curious, not pushy. Get them talking.

❓Key Discovery Questions▼
  • How long have you been here? Where before that?
  • What's your title? What are you responsible for — building or supporting?
  • How many reports? What do they do? Average years of experience?
  • Contract or FT employees in your group?
  • Technologies used?
  • When's the last time you hired? Did you use an agency? Which one? What did you like/dislike?
  • What projects or initiatives over the next 6–12 months?
  • Who else should we get to know here?
  • If there was one skill set you'd like to know about, what would it be?
  • What's your fiscal year? Busiest time? Vendor spend?
👁️

Scan the room for personal items — reasons to call back later. Build a connection point.

📋Before You Leave the Meeting▼
  • If you get a job order: set up intake call with HM + recruiters before you leave
  • Ask: "Who else would be good to meet with?" and ask permission to name-drop
  • Get their cell number for texting
  • After meeting: complete CEIPAL profile immediately
  • Build follow-up plan: active / long-term / passive
🎭 Selling the Process to Hiring Managers
🔥 Do This with ENTHUSIASM Every Time
⚡
Always Use Third-Party Stories

Never tell them what to do — show them what your successful clients do. Let the story do the persuading.

  • "I'd be doing you a disservice if I didn't tell you what's going on in the market."
  • "Companies have stopped waiting for vacancies — they're recruiting nonstop and creating roles for top talent."
  • "You can't compete with Amazon on money or Google on benefits. But you CAN beat them — here's how our other clients do it."
  • "Our clients getting the best results are interviewing candidates we suggest — 1 in 3 get hired."
  • "Our clients getting the best results provide same-day feedback."
📄 Reviewing Candidate Submissions
📋What to Check Before Submitting▼
  • Review resume against JD and your notes from the hiring manager
  • Write up a comparison — don't just forward the resume
  • If not currently working: recruiter must include the reason why
  • If hybrid/onsite: verify they live close or will commute/relocate
  • Check for employment gaps — recruiter must address in write-up
  • Check formatting and spelling
  • Listen to the recruiter's screening recording for communication skills and culture fit
  • Ask recruiter follow-up questions before submitting — you can also call the candidate yourself
🤝 Working with Recruiters
👔 BDM Role
  • Win the job order → hand off to recruiting team
  • Own the client relationship
  • All submissions go through YOU to the client
  • Stay involved: intake call with HM + recruiters
🔎 Recruiter Role
  • Own the candidate relationship
  • Source, screen, submit candidates through you
  • Provide screening recordings and write-ups
  • Follow up next day if no feedback from HM — always copy the recruiter
  • Feedback loop is critical: if client rejects, get detailed reasons and relay to recruiter immediately

  • "How did you come up with that number?"
  • "We could get someone with fewer qualifications at that price."
  • "Contractors drive pricing, not Rylem."

Don't Ask — Tell

Don't ASK for the budget — TELL them the market rate. All submitted rates are just getting off on the right foot — always be willing to ask for more to get a great candidate.

9️⃣ The 9-Touch Follow-Up System
🔁 After Every HM Meeting — Execute This Sequence

It takes 9 touches for someone to truly know you exist. Don't stop at 2.

  1. First meeting ✅
  2. Send thank-you email FROM THE BUILDING (shows urgency — do it before you reach your car)
  3. Send a hand-written card when you return to the office
  4. Send LinkedIn connection request
  5. Mail them a Rylem swag bag
  6. Walk a candidate in to interview (be on-site)
  7. Have your contractor introduce you to the manager over lunch
  8. Take the manager out to lunch
  9. Invite them for a beer or coffee
  10. Send them articles about their business/industry

🎯 Client Interviews — Maximize Your Advantage
💡Advanced Interview Strategies▼
  • Instead of just sending resumes, ask for interview time blocks where they interview whoever you suggest
  • Offer to be on-site to "babysit" — debrief candidates as they finish, debrief with client after, get decisions immediately
  • Ask for exclusives. If not, ask for a 2-week head start
  • These models create priority on the combat board in morning meetings and get more recruiter resources allocated
🤝 Negotiation
🚨
NEVER Release the Information Until the Deal is Plotted

Never tell the candidate they have the job — or tell the client the candidate accepts — without plotting this step first. The minute you release the information, you release the power.

  • Avoid "let me think about it for a day/weekend" — always close NOW
  • Time kills all deals
📝 Resume Review — What to Look For
🔍Evaluating a Candidate's Value▼
  • How did this person generate or save their company money?
  • In what way were the things they built sophisticated, complex, or necessary to keeping business running?
  • "Tell me about your role in this last group. What were you building? How many people on the team?"
  • "Rate yourself among your peers — why?"

🔬 Advanced: The Challenger Sale
📖 The Challenger Sale — by Dixon & Adamson
🔥
The Core Insight

Relationship Builders — the reps everyone assumes are the best — actually perform the worst in complex B2B sales. Challengers outperform every other profile by a wide margin. Being liked doesn't close deals. Teaching something new does.

🏛️ The Three Pillars
Teach
Lead with an insight they didn't know about their own business. "Your 4 open DevOps roles have been posted 47 days — that's costing you $680/day per seat in lost productivity."
Tailor
Frame the message to the person's specific role and pain. VP of Engineering hears delivery risk. CFO hears burn rate.
Take Control
Don't flinch on pricing, push back on process, drive the timeline. "I can have 3 qualified candidates on your desk by Friday."
📊 Commercial Insights — Use These in Conversations
  • Time-to-fill tech roles: 35–60 days — The Resource Company, 2025
  • Cost of unfilled tech position: $500–$700/day in lost productivity — Dice / McKinsey
  • Bad hire costs ~33% of annual salary to replace — Corporate Navigators, 2026
  • Global tech talent shortage: $5.5 trillion in projected losses by 2026 — Second Talent Research
  • Every open day = 20–30% more workload absorbed by remaining team → burnout → more turnover — SHRM
🎯Scenario 1: First Meeting with a VP of Engineering▼
❌ Relationship Builder (Loses)
"Hey Mark, thanks for taking the time. I'd love to learn more about your team and what you're hiring for. We've been in staffing for 18 years and work with a lot of tech companies in your space. If you ever have needs, we'd love to be a resource."
⚠️

You asked them to do all the work. You taught them nothing. You sound like every other staffing rep who calls them 10x a week.

✅ Challenger (Wins)
"Mark, I noticed your team posted 4 Senior DevOps roles on LinkedIn about 6 weeks ago — they're still open. Based on what we're seeing in DFW right now, DevOps engineers with Kubernetes and AWS experience are averaging 42 days to fill, and the best ones are off the market in under 10.

At your company's revenue per head, those 4 empty seats are costing you roughly $2,200 a day in lost delivery capacity. That's $66K just since they went up.

We specialize in getting IT contractors placed in under 2 weeks — not 6. I can have 3 qualified candidates for your top-priority role on your desk by Friday. Worth 20 minutes tomorrow to nail down the requirements?"
💡

Teach: daily dollar cost they didn't calculate. Tailor: delivery capacity (what keeps VPs up at night). Take Control: specific deliverable + specific day.

🔄Scenario 2: Re-Engaging a Dormant Client▼
❌ Relationship Builder (Loses)
"Hi Sarah, it's been a while since we connected. Just wanted to check in and see how things are going. If you have any openings, we'd love to help again."
⚠️

Zero value. Zero insight. This email gets deleted in 2 seconds.

✅ Challenger (Wins)
"Sarah, I saw your company just opened a new office in Austin and posted 12 roles across Finance and IT in the last 3 weeks. When we worked together 2 years ago, your biggest pain point was getting qualified FP&A analysts — took your internal team 8+ weeks.

The Austin finance talent market has tightened since then. FP&A candidates with SaaS experience are commanding 15% more than last year, and the best ones aren't on job boards — they're being approached directly.

We placed 3 FP&A contractors for you in 2024 and all three extended. I've already pulled a short list of Austin-based finance talent that matches your last specs. Want to do a quick call Thursday to see if the requirements have shifted?"
💡

Teach: Austin finance market shifted — higher comp, passive candidates. Tailor: their expansion, their roles, their past pain point. Take Control: already pulled a short list before they asked.

⚔️Scenario 3: Competing Against a Large Agency / VMS▼
❌ Relationship Builder (Loses)
"I know you're working with [Big Agency], but if you ever want to explore other options, we're here. We're very competitive on pricing and would love to earn your business."
⚠️

You positioned yourself as the cheaper backup plan. Zero incentive to disrupt their current vendor.

✅ Challenger (Wins)
"Tom, I'll be direct. I know you're running IT contractors through [Big Agency]'s VMS right now. I'm not going to waste your time talking about rates.

Here's what I want you to think about: your last 5 IT placements through [Big Agency] — how many were still on the job after 90 days? Industry average through large VMS programs is 68% retention. Through specialized firms like ours, it's 91%.

The reason is simple: VMS programs optimize for volume and margin. They throw 50 resumes at a wall. We interview every candidate against your actual tech stack and team culture before you ever see a resume.

That 23% gap in retention? On a $75/hr contractor, one bad placement that churns at 60 days costs you $18,000 in ramp-up time, lost productivity, and restart fees. Multiply that by the 5-6 contractors you cycle per year.

I'm not asking you to rip out your VMS. I'm asking you to give us one role — your hardest-to-fill position — and let the results speak. If we don't outperform, you haven't lost anything. 15 minutes this week to pick the role?"
💡

Teach: 68% vs 91% retention reframes the conversation from rate to cost. Tailor: spoke to their language (tech stack, ramp-up, restart fees). Take Control: removed the big scary ask ("I'm not asking you to rip out your VMS"), proposed one role — low risk, high reward.

💬Quick Talk Tracks — Common Objections▼
🚫 "We're not hiring right now"
"I hear that. But here's what I've been seeing — companies in your space that paused hiring in Q1 are scrambling in Q3 because they didn't build a bench. What if we spent 15 minutes mapping out what your Q3 needs might look like so you're not starting from zero when the green light comes?"
💵 "Just send me your rate card"
"I can do that, but rates without context are meaningless. A $65/hr developer who delivers in week one is cheaper than a $55/hr developer who takes 3 weeks to get productive. Let me understand the role first — 10 minutes — and I'll send you a rate with a candidate attached instead of a PDF."
🏢 "We use our internal team"
"That makes sense for core hires. Quick question though — what's your time-to-fill on contract IT roles specifically? Most internal teams we talk to are hitting 40–50 days on contractors because it's not their core focus. We average 11 days. For your next urgent backfill, it might be worth having us in your back pocket."
🤝 "We already have a staffing vendor"
"I'd be surprised if you didn't. Most of the companies we work with started with 1–2 vendors and added us for a specific reason: speed on hard-to-fill roles. I'm not asking you to replace anyone. I'm asking: what's the one role that's been sitting open the longest? Let me show you what we can do with just that one."
⚡ Rules of Engagement
  1. Always research before you reach out. Know their open roles, recent news, and market conditions. No insight = don't call yet.
  2. Never lead with Rylem. Lead with their problem. Rylem shows up in the solution, not the introduction.
  3. Talk to the person who feels the empty chair. VPs, Directors, team leads — not procurement, not vendor management.
  4. One insight per conversation. Don't dump 5 stats. Pick the ONE thing that makes them stop and rethink.
  5. Drive the timeline. "Thursday at 2" beats "whenever works for you."
  6. Earn the right to push. Teach first → Tailor second → Take Control. The sequence matters.
📊
You're Not Losing to Competitors

You're losing to indecision. 40–60% of pipeline is lost to "no decision." When a prospect goes quiet, assume indecision — not disinterest. Create urgency with market data.
